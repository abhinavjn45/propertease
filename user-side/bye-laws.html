Bye-Laws of Shree Vikas Nagar Co-operative Housing Society Ltd.

Adopted: 15 March 2020 Last Amended: 10 January 2024 Registered with Co-operative Department

1 Definitions

In these bye-laws, unless the context otherwise requires, the following terms shall have the meanings assigned to them:

  • "Society" means Shree Vikas Nagar Co-operative Housing Society Ltd., registered under the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960.
  • "Member" means a person who has been duly admitted to membership of the Society and whose name is entered in the register of members.
  • "Flat" means a separate and self-contained dwelling unit in the building owned by the Society, including the undivided proportionate share in the common areas and facilities.
  • "Managing Committee" means the committee elected by the members to manage the affairs of the Society.
  • "General Body" means all the members of the Society collectively.
  • "Common Areas" means all parts of the property except the flats, including but not limited to staircases, lifts, lobbies, gardens, parking areas, and amenities.

2 Membership

Membership in the Society shall be governed by the following provisions:

2.1 Eligibility for Membership

  • Any person who is a major (18 years or above) and of sound mind may apply for membership.
  • Joint membership is permitted for co-owners of a flat, subject to a maximum of two members per flat.
  • Membership is mandatory for all flat owners in the Society.

2.2 Admission Process

  • Application for membership shall be made in the prescribed form along with the required documents.
  • The Managing Committee shall consider the application within 30 days of receipt.
  • Upon approval, the applicant shall pay the admission fee and share capital as prescribed.
  • Membership becomes effective upon entry of the member's name in the register of members.

2.3 Termination of Membership

Membership shall terminate upon:

  • Transfer or sale of the flat to another person
  • Death of the member (in case of sole membership)
  • Resignation accepted by the Managing Committee
  • Expulsion by the General Body for violation of bye-laws
ℹ️ Important Note
All members must maintain their membership in good standing by paying dues on time and complying with Society rules. Non-compliance may result in penalties or suspension of membership privileges.

3 Rights & Duties of Members

3.1 Rights of Members

Every member shall have the following rights:

  • To attend and vote at General Body meetings
  • To contest elections for the Managing Committee
  • To inspect the books of accounts and records of the Society during office hours
  • To use common areas and facilities as per Society rules
  • To receive notices of meetings and copies of annual reports
  • To nominate a person to succeed to membership in case of death

3.2 Duties of Members

Every member shall:

  • Pay maintenance charges and other dues promptly by the due date
  • Comply with all bye-laws, rules, and regulations of the Society
  • Maintain their flat in good condition and not cause damage to common property
  • Not carry out any structural alterations without prior written permission
  • Not use the flat for any illegal, immoral, or objectionable purposes
  • Not create nuisance or disturbance to other members
  • Inform the Society of any change in contact details within 15 days
  • Cooperate with the Managing Committee in the administration of the Society

4 Managing Committee

The affairs of the Society shall be managed by a Managing Committee elected by the General Body.

4.1 Composition

  • The Managing Committee shall consist of 12 members elected from among the members of the Society.
  • The Committee shall elect from among themselves a Chairman, Vice-Chairman, Secretary, Joint Secretary, and Treasurer.
  • The term of office shall be 2 years from the date of election.

4.2 Powers and Functions

The Managing Committee shall have the power to:

  • Manage and administer the affairs of the Society
  • Prepare annual budgets and fix maintenance charges
  • Maintain and repair common areas and facilities
  • Employ staff and fix their remuneration
  • Open and operate bank accounts
  • Frame rules and regulations for day-to-day functioning
  • Levy penalties for violation of bye-laws
  • Approve or reject applications for membership

4.3 Meetings

  • The Managing Committee shall meet at least once every month.
  • The quorum for meetings shall be one-third of the total members or 5 members, whichever is higher.
  • Decisions shall be taken by majority vote, with the Chairman having a casting vote in case of a tie.

5 General Meetings

5.1 Annual General Meeting

  • The Society shall hold an Annual General Meeting (AGM) within 6 months of the close of the financial year.
  • At least 14 days' notice shall be given to all members.
  • The AGM shall consider the annual report, audited accounts, and election of the Managing Committee.

5.2 Special General Meetings

  • Special General Meetings may be called by the Managing Committee or upon requisition by at least 20% of the members.
  • At least 7 days' notice shall be given specifying the agenda.

5.3 Quorum and Voting

  • The quorum for General Meetings shall be one-fifth of the total members or 25 members, whichever is less.
  • Each member shall have one vote, regardless of the number of flats owned.
  • Voting shall be by show of hands unless a poll is demanded.
  • Proxy voting is not permitted.

6 Maintenance Charges & Funds

6.1 Maintenance Charges

  • Every member shall pay monthly maintenance charges as fixed by the Managing Committee and approved by the General Body.
  • Charges shall be based on the carpet area of the flat and shall cover expenses for upkeep of common areas, staff salaries, utilities, and repairs.
  • Charges are payable by the 10th of each month. Late payment shall attract interest at 18% per annum.

6.2 Sinking Fund

  • The Society shall maintain a Sinking Fund for major repairs and capital expenditure.
  • Members shall contribute to the Sinking Fund as determined by the Managing Committee, typically 0.25% of the flat value per annum.

6.3 Non-Payment of Dues

In case of non-payment of dues for 3 consecutive months:

  • The member's voting rights may be suspended
  • Access to common facilities may be restricted
  • Legal action may be initiated for recovery
  • Interest and penalty charges shall be levied
Payment Information
Maintenance charges can be paid online through bank transfer, UPI, or by cheque at the Society office. Please ensure timely payment to avoid penalties and maintain uninterrupted services.

7 Transfer of Flats

7.1 Transfer Procedure

  • A member wishing to transfer their flat must give written notice to the Society.
  • The transferor must clear all outstanding dues before the transfer.
  • The Society shall issue a No Objection Certificate (NOC) within 30 days of application, subject to clearance of dues.
  • Transfer charges as prescribed by the Managing Committee shall be payable.

7.2 Admission of Transferee

  • The transferee must apply for membership within 3 months of the transfer.
  • The Managing Committee may reject the application only on grounds specified in the Co-operative Societies Act.
  • Upon admission, the transferee shall have all rights and obligations of membership.

7.3 Letting and Licensing

  • Members may let out their flats to tenants with prior intimation to the Society.
  • The member shall remain responsible for payment of all dues and compliance with bye-laws.
  • Tenants must comply with all Society rules and regulations.
  • The Society reserves the right to refuse entry to tenants who violate rules.

8 Dispute Resolution

8.1 Internal Resolution

  • All disputes between members or between members and the Society shall first be referred to the Managing Committee.
  • The Committee shall attempt to resolve the dispute through mediation and conciliation.
  • The Committee's decision shall be communicated in writing within 30 days.

8.2 Arbitration

  • If internal resolution fails, the dispute may be referred to arbitration as per the Co-operative Societies Act.
  • The arbitrator shall be appointed by mutual consent or by the Co-operative Court.
  • The arbitrator's decision shall be final and binding on all parties.

8.3 Penalties for Violations

The Managing Committee may impose the following penalties for violation of bye-laws:

  • Warning letter for first-time minor violations
  • Fine up to ₹10,000 for repeated or serious violations
  • Suspension of membership privileges
  • Recommendation to the General Body for expulsion in extreme cases
Legal Framework
These bye-laws are framed under the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Act, 1960 and the Maharashtra Co-operative Societies Rules, 1961. In case of any conflict, the provisions of the Act and Rules shall prevail.
